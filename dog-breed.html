Breed is determined by behavior, size, look, activities, ancestral lines, temperament and so on. These categories are even not enough. There are local, national and global organisations too who form their own opinion bending or conforming the set rules. Genealogical tests are also used in some rare cases to figure out a breed. Not only a novice but also a professional dog expert may get deceived by the appearances and have a wrong opinion in this regard. Breed standards mostly relies on circumstantial factors rather than scientific basis. This standard often changes depending on the organizational requirements.

There are 210 breeds we own today of which the AKC recognizes 194. A breed standard helps us place a certain distinction on a category of dog. It also assists the judges in determining the true class and characteristics of a competing dog. History plays its unique part here as the breeders, over the centuries, streamline a connection between two separate types of dogs and start a new generation which is often introduced with a different name.
